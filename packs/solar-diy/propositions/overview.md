### Pack-Level Facts

- This is a composite ExpertPack combining a product (technology) sub-pack and a process (how-to) sub-pack for residential solar installation.
- A complete residential DIY solar installation requires knowledge in four domains: electrical engineering (string sizing, NEC code), construction (roof work, racking), permitting (AHJ + utility processes), and project management (7 phases, 3–9 months).
- The two most dangerous activities in DIY solar are service panel work (live 200A bus) and roof work (fall risk); both have killed homeowners who took shortcuts.
- A 6–10 kW solar system in 2026 costs $20,000–$30,000 contractor-installed or $10,000–$18,000 DIY (equipment + materials).
- The 30% federal Investment Tax Credit (ITC) applies to the full installed cost including labor, equipment, and permitting for homeowner-installed or contractor-installed systems.
- Net metering (selling excess power back to the grid) is regulated by state public utility commissions and policies have been deteriorating in some states (California NEM 3.0 reduced export rates ~75%).
- Residential solar panels produce 440–500W each in 2026; a 10 kW system requires approximately 20–23 panels.
- String sizing — calculating voltage limits for series-connected panels — is the most technically critical design step; errors can damage inverters or create fire hazards.
- The utility interconnection process (4–12 weeks, sometimes 6 months) is almost always the critical path — longer than the building permit and the physical installation combined.
- The federal ITC is claimed in the tax year the system is commissioned and placed in service; it cannot be claimed in advance of operation.
- All leading residential battery systems in 2026 use LFP (lithium iron phosphate) chemistry, which is safer and longer-lived than earlier NMC lithium batteries.
- Solar panels are energized by daylight — DC conductors should be treated as live during all installation and maintenance activities.
- Most US residential jurisdictions allow homeowner-pulled permits for owner-occupied homes; some require a licensed electrical contractor for the AC connection.
- Solar ROI depends primarily on: local electricity rate, local peak sun hours, net metering policy, and whether battery storage is included.
- Panel degradation at 0.5%/year means a 400W panel produces approximately 362W (90.5% of original) at year 25.
